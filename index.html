Fertilizers & Pesticides

Find information on fertilizers and pesticides for better crop management.

Organic Fertilizers

Description: Natural fertilizers derived from plant or animal sources.

Benefits: Improves soil health, reduces chemical dependency, and enhances crop quality.

Chemical Fertilizers

Description: Synthetic fertilizers with specific nutrient compositions (NPK).

Benefits: Quick nutrient availability, high yield potential, but requires careful application.

Bio-Pesticides

Description: Pest control agents derived from natural materials like bacteria or plants.

Benefits: Environmentally friendly, targets specific pests, and safe for beneficial insects.

Chemical Pesticides

Description: Synthetic chemicals for pest, weed, and disease control.

Benefits: Effective against a wide range of pests, but requires proper usage to avoid environmental harm.

Integrated Pest Management (IPM)

Description: Holistic approach combining biological, cultural, and chemical methods.

Benefits: Sustainable pest control, reduces pesticide resistance, and maintains ecological balance.

Soil Amendments

Description: Materials added to soil to improve its physical properties.

Benefits: Enhances soil structure, water retention, and nutrient availability.
